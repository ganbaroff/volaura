## THE POST (copy-paste ready)

I managed 200+ volunteers at the CIS Games in Ganja last year.

5,000 athletes. 30+ venues. Government coordination across multiple agencies. And 200 volunteers I was personally responsible for.

On day 3, one of my best volunteers came to me almost in tears.

She spoke fluent English, had organized university events for 3 years, and had excellent communication skills. We had assigned her to check parking passes at the back entrance. For 12 hours.

I asked the coordination team: why?

"We didn't know she could do more."

That's the moment this started.

---

Before Ganja, there was COP29. 170 milestones. 80 coordination meetings. 15,000+ guests.

Before COP29, there was WUF13 — the UN World Urban Forum. 220+ volunteers. 35 coordinators under me.

10 years of this. The pattern was always the same:

Talented volunteers show up. Nobody knows what they can actually do. The best ones get assigned to the worst tasks. By day 3, the coordinator is firefighting instead of coordinating.

The problem isn't the volunteers. The problem is that nobody measures what they can do before they show up.

---

After CIS Games, I took my AI assistant and said: "Build me a system that measures volunteer competencies the way real psychometric tests work."

My role was the same as at CIS Games — architecture, quality control, and making sure the right capabilities get assigned to the right problems. Whether I'm directing 35 coordinators or an AI team, the skill is the same: knowing what needs to be built and making sure it gets built right.

The platform is called Volaura.

Organizations don't browse profiles — they search by verified skills. Volunteers don't just apply — they prove what they can do.

If my CIS Games coordinator had Volaura, she would have seen in 10 seconds that the volunteer at the parking entrance was the most qualified person on the team for guest relations.

---

I'm building this from Baku. No VC funding. No technical co-founder. What I do have: 10 years managing the biggest events Azerbaijan has hosted, and a network of organizations who need this.

If you're organizing an event with 50+ volunteers in 2026 and want to stop assigning your best people to parking passes — DM me.
