## Context: what actually needs counsel

The Stripe Atlas mega-plan §4 flagged three things that can't be DIY'd:

1. **CFC (Controlled Foreign Corporation) exposure** — once the CEO personally owns >50% of Delaware C-Corp and lives in Azerbaijan, some scenarios trigger Subpart F or GILTI-adjacent treatment on the AZ side. Needs cross-border specialist.
2. **Dividend withholding** — US-AZ has NO tax treaty currently. So US → AZ dividends face full 30% WHT with no treaty relief. A lawyer might structure around this (e.g., management fees, IP licensing from AZ to DE) but that also has transfer-pricing risk.
3. **83(b) election** — Stripe Atlas auto-files this for founders (good news, de-risks our deadlines.md entry) BUT the filing requires SSN or ITIN at the moment of filing, and the CEO has neither. Counsel needed to sequence ITIN application vs Atlas filing correctly.

Stripe Atlas explicitly says they do not provide tax or legal advice. So we need someone beyond Atlas.

## Recommended engagement model

For minimum viable counsel before Stripe Atlas filing:

1. **Tier 1 firm (Faison or Bosin) — flat-fee DE formation package: $1,500–$3,500**
   - Delivers: entity formed, bylaws, restricted stock, vesting, IP assignment, 83(b) filing coordination, founder share grant
   - Replaces what Stripe Atlas Premium promises but with a named human attorney

2. **Tier 2 firm (Caplin & Drysdale or Cross Border Tax Law) — one-hour CFC/WHT consult: $500–$1,000**
   - Delivers: concrete answer on whether to route revenue through AZ or direct to DE, whether to establish AZ as a cost centre subsidiary, transfer-pricing policy draft
   - Output is a 2–3 page memo the CEO files for future reference

3. **Tier 4 firm (Kesikli or CEO-network) — AZ compliance coordination: $300–$800**
   - Delivers: SADPP notification, AZ tax-return CFC disclosure framework, bilateral management fee documentation if routing through AZ

**Total envelope:** $2,300–$5,300 for full pre-Atlas and post-Atlas year-one counsel. 
Compare to Stripe Atlas Premium fee differential ($500 → $2,000 = +$1,500): Premium gives you *some* of item (1) plus a lawyer consult *included* but NOT (2) or (3). Either path is defensible. The Tier 1+2+4 combined route gives better cross-border depth; Atlas Premium is simpler if CEO wants to minimize decisions.
