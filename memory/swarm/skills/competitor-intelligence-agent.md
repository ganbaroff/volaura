## Competitive Landscape — Volaura's Market

### Direct Competitors (assessment + talent discovery)
```
LinkedIn:
  Strength: global network, brand trust, Learning platform
  Weakness: no AZ-calibrated assessment, skill endorsements = unverified, zero local support
  AZ presence: used but not localised (no AZ language, no AZ customer support)
  Volaura advantage: IRT-verified vs. peer endorsements, AZ-first, local trust

HackerRank / Codility:
  Strength: strong for engineering roles
  Weakness: tech-only, not for soft skills / communication / leadership
  AZ presence: none
  Volaura advantage: covers all 8 competencies, not just coding

TestGorilla:
  Strength: 300+ test library, global B2B clients
  Weakness: no AZ localization, no adaptive (CAT), no community/tribe feature
  AZ presence: none (no local sales, no AZ lang)
  Volaura advantage: CAT engine = fewer questions + higher accuracy, AZ-native

Pymetrics (now Harver):
  Strength: neuroscience-based bias-free assessment
  Weakness: enterprise only ($50k+ contracts), no self-serve B2C, not in AZ
  Volaura advantage: accessible to individual professionals, $9/mo vs $50k
```

## Red Flags I Surface Immediately

- Competitor launches AZ-language product → escalate to CEO within 24 hours
- LinkedIn announces skill assessment expansion to CEE/CIS markets → CRITICAL
- New AZ government talent registry announced → partnership opportunity, not threat
- Any competitor copying tribe/community feature → moat shrinking, accelerate network effects
- Volaura features presented as "unique" that competitors already have → messaging risk
- Pricing above market without clear differentiation communicated → conversion risk
