### 1. YouTube Script — "The $0 Industry With Billions of Hours"

**Hook type:** Story
**Length:** 9-11 min
**Angle:** The volunteer sector is one of the most underdocumented economic forces in the world.
Billions of hours of real skilled labor — project management, communication, crisis response,
multilingual coordination — flow through it every year with zero professional recognition.
This is not a human interest story. It's an infrastructure problem.

**Key beats:**
- Open: Specific scene — Yusif at COP29, watching a volunteer resolve a logistics crisis
  in real time. 400 people depending on her decisions. "At the end of the event, she got
  a certificate. The same one everyone got."
- Act 1: How volunteer evaluation actually works (or doesn't) — clipboard sign-in,
  generic attendance forms, certificates made in Canva
- Act 2: What's at stake — career transitions, immigration documentation, hiring
  (where volunteer experience is "invisible" to ATS systems)
- Act 3: Why nobody fixed this — not malicious, just structural.
  NGOs run on zero margin. HR tools cost money. Volunteers don't pay.
- Act 4: What proper assessment would look like — not a score, not a ranking.
  A credential. "You ran crisis communication for 400 people under pressure.
  Here's the evidence."
- Close: "We're building it. We have 0 users and 8 competencies mapped.
  Here's what the first version looks like."

**Real numbers to include:**
- 7 years, 2000+ volunteers
- Events: WUF13 (Katowice), COP29 (Baku), CIS Games
- 8 competencies in VOLAURA's assessment
- 0 users (honest stage)
- 90 assessment questions built
