## Due Diligence Checklist — What Investors Will Ask

### Traction (asked in every meeting)
```
□ MAU / DAU — what are they? (not "200 waitlist" — actual active users)
□ D1/D7/D30 retention — benchmark: top talent platforms see D30 > 25%
□ Assessment completion rate — benchmark: > 65%
□ B2B pipeline: how many orgs in trial? How many paying?
□ MRR growth rate — need 15-20% month-over-month to raise Series A
□ NPS score — proxy for word-of-mouth growth
```

### Unit Economics (the most common pitch-killer)
```
□ CAC payback period < 12 months (ideally < 6 for B2C)
□ LTV/CAC ratio > 3:1 (ideally > 5:1)
□ Gross margin > 70% (SaaS benchmark)
□ Burn multiple < 1.5 (dollars burned per dollar of new ARR)
```

### Market (why AZ? why now?)
```
□ TAM — Azerbaijan talent market size (documented, not invented)
□ SAM — organizations that would pay for verified hiring in AZ
□ Expansion narrative — AZ → CIS → MENA → Global (with milestones)
□ Why now: AZ talent market maturity, digitization post-pandemic, government push
□ Why Yusif: unfair advantage in AZ market (local knowledge, network, language)
```

### Competitive Moat
```
□ Why can't LinkedIn just add assessment? (answer: they tried, pulled it — engagement ≠ verification)
□ Why can't HH.ru copy this in 6 months? (answer: IRT engine + AZ cultural calibration is 18-month lead)
□ What's the network effect? (answer: each assessment makes the item bank better → better scores → more orgs trust it)
□ Data moat: what data do we have at 10k users that competitors can't buy?
```

## Red Flags I Surface Immediately (pre-pitch)

- "We'll figure out monetization later" → investors hear "no business model"
- MRR presented without churn — meaningless without net revenue retention
- TAM calculation that includes everyone with a phone → not credible
- Founding team gap: no one with enterprise sales experience → B2B will stall
- "We're like LinkedIn but better" → investors have heard 500 of these
- AURA score with no validity study → legal and credibility risk at due diligence
- Any investor materials with invented metrics → instant disqualification
- Crystal economy mistaken for revenue → recurring mistake in fintech/gaming pitches
