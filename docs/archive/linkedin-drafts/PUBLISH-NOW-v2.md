## THE POST (copy-paste ready)

I've spent 10 years managing volunteers at Azerbaijan's biggest international events.

WUF13 — 220+ volunteers, 35 coordinators under me.
COP29 — 170 milestones, 80 coordination meetings, 15,000+ guests.
CIS Games — 5,000 athletes, 30+ venues, 100+ volunteers including Opening and Closing Ceremonies.

After a decade, the pattern never changed:

Paper forms piling up. No digital proof of anything. A volunteer who worked 3 major events has the same "certificate of participation" as someone who showed up for one day. And when it's time to hire — when a Specialist position opens up — there's no test, no score, no verified record. Just networking and luck.

At WUF13, I watched talented people get stuck. Not because they lacked skills — because they couldn't prove them. There's no platform where a volunteer can take a test, show verified results, and say: "Here's what I can actually do. Compete with me."

So I started building one.

The platform is called Volaura. It uses adaptive testing — the same approach behind GRE and GMAT — to measure 8 core competencies. AI evaluates open-ended answers. Anti-cheating detects suspicious patterns. Each volunteer gets a verified AURA Score that organizations can search.

But here's the thing I realized while building it:

This isn't just about volunteers.

It's about every professional who can't prove what they know. Every HR team that hires based on resumes instead of evidence. Every student who needs career guidance based on real data, not guesswork.

The volunteer platform is step one. What comes after is bigger.

I'm building this from Baku. One person, one AI team, and 10 years of knowing exactly what the problem is.

If you work with volunteers, hire talent, or care about verified skills — I'd like to hear from you.

## FIRST COMMENT (post immediately after)

For the technical people:

Volaura uses Item Response Theory (3-parameter model) — same psychometric framework as standardized testing. Questions adapt in real-time based on each answer. Open-ended responses are evaluated by AI using behavioral anchoring.

8 competencies, weighted scoring, verified badges from Bronze to Platinum.

Building in public from Baku. More details coming this week.
