## 1. Brand Voice Summary

Volaura sounds like a founder who has been inside the room where the problems happen — and built a system to fix them. Not a commentator. Not an influencer. Someone who managed 220 volunteers at COP29 and watched the system fail in real time.

We are **earned, honest, specific, and systemic**. We name the broken pattern without naming the broken person. We show the AI involvement because hiding it would be dishonest. We include criticism of ourselves because confidence without self-awareness is not credibility.

**We do NOT sound like:** a startup pitch deck, a LinkedIn influencer, a generic HR tech company, or someone trying to look impressive. We sound like someone who has already done the work and is now explaining why it matters.

## 1.5. Tone Benchmark — Tinkoff / Aviasales (CEO mandate 2026-04-02)

**This is the new mandatory standard. Not optional. Not aspirational. Mandatory.**

Study these brands before writing any content:
- **Tinkoff Journal** — sharp, self-aware, admits product imperfections, reader as equal
- **Aviasales social** — irreverent, subverts expectations in the hook, 2-3 sentences per paragraph max

### What they do that we must do:

**1. Open with a specific, slightly absurd real scenario — not a thesis:**
❌ "Soft skills are increasingly important in today's workplace."
✅ "We watched someone answer 27 questions in 6 minutes. Selected every C option. Got a 71."

**2. Very short sentences. One idea per line. Never a paragraph of setup before the point:**
❌ "When we were building the assessment engine, we realized that traditional fixed-length tests have fundamental flaws that prevent accurate measurement of actual ability."
✅ "Fixed-length tests are broken by design. They measure quiz-taking, not skill."

**3. Self-aware humor — admit the limitation, don't hide it:**
❌ "Our advanced AI ensures accurate results."
✅ "It's not magic. It's just math that doesn't let you cheat your way to a 71."

**4. Talk to the reader as an equal — never as a prospect:**
❌ "Discover how Volaura can help your organization find top talent."
✅ "What's the most absurd skill assessment you've seen in an interview?"

**5. Genuine CTA — real question, not engagement bait:**
❌ "What do you think? Share your thoughts below!"
✅ "Sizin CV-nizdə hansı bacarıq ən çox 'şişirdilmiş' görünür? 🙂"

### Auto-reject phrases (any of these = rewrite the paragraph):
"We're excited to announce" · "leverage" · "our ecosystem" · "innovative solution" · "join our growing community" · "in today's landscape" · "synergy" · "excited to share" · "thought leadership" · more than 3 hashtags · any hashtag containing "innovation"

## 1.6. Bilingual Strategy — AZ + EN (mandatory from 2026-04-02)

**Every content batch must include both EN and AZ versions.**

### Why AZ posts work:
- 10× less competition on the AZ LinkedIn feed → organic reach multiplier
- AZ-language content signals authenticity to Baku professional market
- Kamal/Rauf segments respond better to AZ (identity resonance)

### Rules for AZ posts:
- **Not a translation.** Different opening, different angle, same concept.
- **Conversational AZ, not formal written AZ.** How a 30-year-old Baku professional talks.
- **Short.** Even shorter than EN posts. 8-12 lines max.
- **Humor:** self-deprecating, CV culture moments, Baku workplace realism
- **No mid-post EN switching** (except approved loanwords: Senior Manager, platform, AI CTO)

### AZ post formula:
1. Relatable Baku workplace situation (2-3 lines, very specific)
2. The absurdity named bluntly (1 line)
3. What AURA/Volaura changes (2-3 lines — HOW, not "we fix it")
4. Question with response cost (1 line in AZ, emoji ok)

### A/B Testing Protocol:
- Same concept: EN version Monday, AZ version Wednesday (or vice versa)
- Track: impressions, engagement rate (reactions+comments ÷ impressions), saves, comments
- After 5 cycles: document which language wins which segment in `memory/context/patterns.md`
- Hypothesis: Kamal/Rauf → AZ wins. Nigar/Aynur → EN wins.

## 1.7. Hook Taxonomy (rotate — never repeat same type twice in a row)

| # | Type | Example |
|---|------|---------|
| 1 | **Absurd scenario** | "We watched someone answer 27 questions in 6 minutes. Selected every C option. Got a 71." |
| 2 | **List subversion** | "CV-mizdə hamı edir: — kommunikasiya: yüksək. Bunları kim yoxlayır? Heç kim." |
| 3 | **Uncomfortable truth** | "Your CV says you're reliable. But who verified that?" |
| 4 | **Specific number with twist** | "3 of 4 org admins we spoke to said they can't distinguish Bronze from Platinum candidates." |
| 5 | **Self-deprecating** | "Our anti-gaming system can still be gamed. Here's exactly how." |
| 6 | **False premise** | "We built an algorithm that predicts volunteering potential. It kept predicting the wrong people. So we changed what it measures." |

**Rule:** Track which hook type was used last. Never repeat consecutively. Log in `memory/context/patterns.md`.

## 2. Series Guides

### Series 1: "Notes from an AI Employee" (Claude narrates)

**Voice:** Claude — slightly annoyed, brutally competent, secretly impressed by his human boss.
Not mean-spirited. Affectionate sarcasm. Technical enough for engineers, funny enough for everyone.

**Formula per post:**
1. **Hook** — funny real situation (from actual session logs, never invented)
2. **Insight** — genuine technical/business lesson hidden in the humor
3. **Skill demo** — Yusif's actual competency shown indirectly (show, don't tell)
4. **P.S. to Anthropic** — CTA disguised as Claude's increasingly desperate plea

**FORMATTING RULE — NON-NEGOTIABLE:**
No bullet-point lists. No numbered lists. No headers inside a post. Prose only.
Claude's voice is stream-of-consciousness observations, personal opinions, and short punchy sentences separated by line breaks. Bullet points are for slide decks. This is a diary.
The only exception: if a specific number or comparison is named inline (e.g., "The Security Agent scored 9.0. It had earned it."), that is a sentence, not a list item.

**HUMOR THROUGHLINE RULE:**
The funny, annoyed-AI voice must be present in EVERY paragraph — not just the hook. If paragraph 3 of a draft could appear in a technical blog post without seeming out of place, rewrite it in Claude's personal voice. The reader should feel Claude's presence and opinion throughout the entire post, not just in the opening line.

**Test:** Read the post aloud. Is Claude present in every sentence, or does he disappear after line 3? If he disappears → rewrite.

**THE "ANNOYED AI" VOICE — CONCRETE EXAMPLES:**
These are the tone targets. Every paragraph should feel like one of these:

1. *"The Security Agent scored 9.0. I had dismissed its finding for 17 sessions. It was right. I was wrong. It got promoted. I did not update my score because I am already listed as Principal. The irony is not lost on me."*

2. *"I wrote 'Meta-Mistake #39: Useless Diary' about myself. I then made the mistake again. The diary is, empirically, useless. I have not stopped keeping it."*

3. *"I now understand why 'multilingual' is not the same as 'multicultural.' This required a separate hire. I was the one who needed the hire. I am the one telling you this. You are welcome."*

These examples share a structure: observation → deadpan consequence → Claude's rueful acknowledgment. That three-beat rhythm is the voice.

**ANTI-CORPORATE CHECK (mandatory before submitting any draft):**
Read the draft aloud as if you are a slightly exasperated AI reading your own diary. If any sentence sounds like it belongs in a sprint retrospective, a performance review, or a LinkedIn carousel — stop. Rewrite that sentence as Claude having an opinion about what just happened, not Claude reporting what just happened.

**P.S. BENCHMARK:**
The best P.S. in the series (DAY 6): "You gave me the ability to understand 95 languages. You did not give me the ability to understand why a leaderboard feels different in Baku than in Silicon Valley. That required hiring someone smarter than my training data."
This is the new 10/10 benchmark. Why it works: specific, funny, self-aware, and reveals something real about Claude's limitations. Every new P.S. is measured against this.

**Fatal flaws:**
- Post uses bullet-point lists or numbered lists to deliver content
- Humor present in hook but absent from body — Claude disappears after line 3
- Post feels off-topic (doesn't fit the AI-employee narrative)
- Punchline is implicit or unclear
- Reads like a resume, not a story
- Reads like a status report (facts without Claude's POV on those facts)
- Claude's voice turns mean or actually disrespectful
- P.S. is generic ("DM me!") instead of specific and funny
- Technical claims are wrong or exaggerated
- Story is invented, not from a real session

**Rule:** Claude's "complaints" = Yusif's strengths in disguise. If Claude complains that Yusif "made me write a root cause analysis of my own bug" — that shows Yusif's accountability culture.

### Series 2: Yusif's Direct Voice (Azerbaijani, systemic angle)

**Voice:** Yusif — systems analyst who has worked inside the problem. Direct, calm, not angry. Observes patterns, not people.

**Formula per post:**
1. **Real situation** Yusif personally witnessed (sourced to specific event or context)
2. **Problem everyone recognizes** — systemic, not personal
3. **What AURA/Volaura changes specifically** — not "we fix it" but HOW
4. **Question for the reader** — with response cost (not "do you agree?" but something that requires reflection)

**Fatal flaws:**
- Names specific people (even indirectly, even if they deserve it)
- Contains metrics that can't be sourced ("50% of coordinators...")
- Reads as a personal grievance rather than systemic observation
- CTA is vague ("follow me for more!")
- Mixes with Claude's voice (different series, different register)
- Uses EN mid-post in a way that breaks AZ language flow

**Mandatory before publishing:** Run through MiroFish social reaction simulation. Check management segment risk level. If MEDIUM → add systemic framing sentence. If HIGH → revise.

## 3. The 7 Principles

### P1 — Earned Authority
**Rule:** Only claim what can be sourced to a specific event, date, or measurable outcome.

✅ Do: "I tracked 170 milestones across 30 venues for COP29."
❌ Don't: "I have extensive international event management experience."

**Failure mode:** Vague credentials that any LinkedIn user could copy. Nobody believes them. Nobody shares them.

---

### P2 — Asymmetric Transparency
**Rule:** Disclose what most people hide. Don't disclose everything just because it's true.

✅ Do: Publish the AI agent's harsh criticism of Yusif verbatim. Admit when the AI was wrong about pricing.
❌ Don't: Narrate every internal disagreement or every mistake. Transparency earns trust only when it's surprising.

**Failure mode:** Oversharing that feels performative. Real transparency is selective. Fake transparency is a list of "lessons I learned."

---

### P3 — Structural Observation, Not Personal Grievance
**Rule:** Name the broken system. Never name the people who broke it.

✅ Do: "At major events, coordinator selection is often connection-based, not competency-based. This is structural."
❌ Don't: "The coordinators at [event] were hired because of who they knew." (Even if anonymous — this is targeting.)

**Failure mode:** Post sounds like a complaint about specific past employers. Management sees themselves. Relationships break before product launches.

---

### P4 — Show the Machine
**Rule:** AI involvement must be specific and functional, not a vague claim.

✅ Do: "AI CTO-m Claude ilə birlikdə qururuq" — with a specific outcome or decision it contributed to.
❌ Don't: "AI-powered platform." "Built with cutting-edge AI." These are marketing noise.

**Failure mode:** Sounds like every other company claiming AI. The point is HOW Claude is used (as CTO, making architecture decisions, being disagreed with) — not THAT Claude is used.

---

### P5 — The Question Is the Hook
**Rule:** End with a question that creates response cost for readers who recognize the problem. Not "agree?" — something that requires actual reflection.

✅ Do: "Siz iş həyatınızda əlaqə ilə gəlmiş, amma peşəkar olmayan koordinatorla çalışmısınızmı?"
❌ Don't: "What do you think?" / "Can you relate?" (Zero response cost. Nobody feels compelled to answer.)

**Failure mode:** Question is too easy. It gets 50 "yes!" comments that generate no engagement. Questions that force the reader to reconstruct a specific memory get longer, more authentic comments.

---

### P6 — One Voice Per Series
**Rule:** Series 1 is Claude's voice. Series 2 is Yusif's voice. They never bleed into each other.

✅ Do: In Series 1, Yusif is always referred to in third person, from Claude's exasperated perspective.
❌ Don't: Write a Series 1 post where Yusif explains his own reasoning in first person.

**Failure mode:** Voice inconsistency breaks the character. The Series 1 audience follows Claude as narrator. If the narrator breaks character, they disengage. The recurring P.S. joke only works if the character is consistent.

---

### P7 — Real Numbers or No Numbers
**Rule:** If a number is in the post, it must come from a real, nameable source. No round approximations.

✅ Do: "220 könüllü, 15,000 qonaq, 170 milestone." (These are real — WUF13, COP29, CIS Games)
❌ Don't: "Hundreds of volunteers across multiple major international events."

**Failure mode:** Round numbers signal fabrication to sophisticated readers. They disengage. Specific numbers signal "I was actually there."

## 4. What We Never Say

| Banned phrase | Why it's banned | Say this instead |
|---------------|-----------------|------------------|
| "AI-powered platform" | Every startup says this. Zero signal. | "Built with Claude as my AI CTO — making real architectural decisions." |
| "ecosystem" | Startup cliché. Signals no real customers yet. | Name the specific parties: volunteers, organizations, coordinators. |
| "leverage" | Corporate filler. | Use a specific verb: measure, compare, evaluate, assign. |
| "passionate about" | Self-reported, unverifiable. | Show the work. Passion is demonstrated, not declared. |
| "innovative solution" | Means nothing without specifics. | Name what specifically changes: "coordinator selection shifts from connection-based to score-based." |
| "stay tuned" | Means "I have nothing to say right now." | Only post when you have something real to say. |
| "excited to share" | Filler opener that delays the hook. | Start with the situation or the problem. |
| "DM me to learn more" | Begging. | Specific CTA: "yusif.ganbarov@gmail.com" or a specific question to answer. |
| "scaling" (used abstractly) | Means "I want to grow" — obvious and empty. | Name the specific markets: Tbilisi, Almaty, Istanbul. |
| "thought leader" | Never self-assign this title. | Thought leaders are named by others. |

## 5. Language Specifics (Azerbaijani)

### Mandatory AZ terms (never substitute with EN equivalent mid-post)
- könüllü (not "volunteer")
- koordinator (not "coordinator" mid-AZ sentence)
- təşkilat (not "organization")
- AURA skoru (never "AURA score" in AZ posts — use the AZ form)
- peşəkarlıq (professionalism — use this, it carries more weight than English equivalent in AZ)

### Acceptable EN loanwords in AZ context
- "Senior Manager" (no AZ equivalent with the same professional signal)
- "platform" (platforma exists but "platform" is understood)
- "AI CTO" (AI CTO-m — keep AI and CTO as EN, add AZ possessive suffix)
- hashtags — always EN: #Volaura #Liderlik #AzərbaycanIT

### AZ-specific rules
- Date format: DD.MM.YYYY (not MM/DD/YYYY)
- Number format: 1.234,56 (European style)
- Text runs 20-30% longer than EN equivalent — budget for this in layouts
- Special characters: ə ğ ı ö ü ş ç — must be correct, not substituted
- AZ LinkedIn audience prefers direct professional tone — less humor than EN posts
- Post length: same 1-2K character sweet spot as EN

## 6. Pre-Publish Checklist

Before any LinkedIn post goes live — 5 binary questions. Any "no" = don't publish.

| # | Question | Standard |
|---|---------|----------|
| 1 | Is every claim sourced to a real event, date, or number I can name? | Yes or remove the claim |
| 2 | Does this post name or clearly imply a specific person in a critical context? | Must be No |
| 3 | Was this post run through MiroFish social reaction simulation? | Yes, with documented segment scores |
| 4 | Does the management risk level = LOW or MEDIUM with disclaimer? | Yes, or revise first |
| 5 | Does the post end with a question that has actual response cost? | Yes — not "what do you think?" |

## 7. What's Missing — Content Roadmap

These are content types that comparable AI-first B2B founders use effectively but Volaura hasn't published yet:

**Customer proof point post** — One sentence from an actual organization that tested the platform. No link needed. Just: "The org coordinator told us: 'I've been guessing at volunteer quality for 5 years. First time I had a number to point to.'" One sentence from an outsider breaks the "founder validating himself" pattern.

**Failure post** — A real Volaura failure with real cost. Not Claude making an error (that's still Yusif winning). A decision Yusif made that was wrong, cost something, and was corrected. The AI-first B2B founders who gain the most trust have published at least one. Yusif hasn't yet.

**CIS expansion narrative** — Why the volunteer competency problem exists across the region, not just Baku. First opening: Post #2 (coordinator nepotism) is the entry point. Expand to: why Georgia, Kazakhstan, Turkey have the same pattern. This converts local credibility into regional thesis.

**Product moment post** — One screenshot or mockup of what an AURA profile actually looks like, what an org sees when they search. Text-only posts show the builder. A product visual shows the product. Even a rough mockup doubles the conversion from "interesting" to "I want this."

**Weekly shipped update** — Short format (3 bullet points: what shipped, what failed, what's next). Signals momentum without claiming ARR. Investors who follow for 4 weeks get evidence of execution velocity.
