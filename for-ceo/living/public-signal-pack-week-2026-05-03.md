### Post 2 — Why energy-adaptive (Thursday)

A standard skills test is brutal for people whose brains run on dopamine math.

If you're tired, every extra item is noise. If you're sharp, ten items is information. So we built the assessment to read your self-reported energy and adjust:

- Full energy: up to 20 items per competency, tight precision threshold
- Mid energy: up to 12 items, medium threshold
- Low energy: up to 5 items, lighter threshold

Same engine. Same scoring model. Different path. The score is anchored in the same item-response model regardless of which path you took.

This isn't a marketing line. It's in the code. It runs.

`volaura.app`

Five lead types, four leads each. Each row in the sheet you'll build on Tuesday gets: lead type, name, organisation, public link, why-they-care, message-angle.

### Lead type A — AZ HR consultants and small recruiting firms

- **Why they care:** they manually vet candidates with stale CVs every week. A free, mathematically-anchored skills score reduces their first-pass screening cost.
- **Message angle:** "you spend hours filtering CVs that lie — here's a free score that doesn't."

### Lead type B — International talent-acquisition managers (SaaS / remote-first)

- **Who to target:** TA managers at remote-first SaaS companies hiring in Eastern Europe / Caucasus, talent-ops leaders at companies actively hiring junior-to-mid English-speaking remote engineers.
- **Why they care:** AURA's English-proficiency score is one of eight competencies and is IRT-based and adaptive, not self-rated. That's the gap they fight every quarter.
- **Message angle:** "your hardest filter is English-as-a-real-skill — we measure it adaptively, not with a 5-minute Zoom."

### Lead type C — ADHD coaches and neurodivergent-talent advocates

- **Who to target:** ADHD coaches with public LinkedIn presence, neurodivergent-career consultants, leaders of neurodiversity-at-work communities.
- **Why they care:** energy-adaptive assessment respects clients' real-day variability. Most platforms penalise ADHD users with rigid timed tests.
- **Message angle:** "your clients fail standard skills tests because the format is the test, not the skill — we built one that adapts."

### Lead type D — University career-office staff in Azerbaijan + region

- **Who to target:** career office directors at ADA, BHOS, Khazar, Baku State, Baku Higher Oil School, plus a few regional AZ universities.
- **Why they care:** they place graduates into orgs that increasingly want verified skills. A free assessment gives their students a portable credential anchor.

### Lead type E — Skills-based hiring advocates and writers

- **Who to target:** writers covering skills-based hiring on LinkedIn / Substack / X, journalists covering HR-tech in Eastern Europe, founders of competing skills-platforms (yes — the smart ones share research).
- **Why they care:** professional audience. They want concrete proof of skills-based hiring working, not another vibes platform. Energy-adaptive psychometrics + Constitution-level shame-free design is a usable case study.
- **Message angle:** "I'm not asking for coverage. I'm asking if energy-adaptive psychometrics is interesting enough to take ten minutes of your time. Try it, tell me what's wrong."
