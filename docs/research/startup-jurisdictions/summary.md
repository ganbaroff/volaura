## Ranked Recommendations

### #1 Georgia VZP LLC
**Why:** Best tax efficiency for an IT SaaS + stackable non-dilutive grants + low capex + EU-adjacent reputation.

- 0% corporate tax on foreign-sourced IT revenue (VZP status)
- 5% dividend tax on distribution (effective blended ~5% if all profit distributed)
- GITA Matching Grants: up to 100K GEL (~$37K), co-financing usually 50/50
- Banking: TBC / Bank of Georgia, USD+EUR accounts, non-resident-friendly
- Setup: LLC (SPS/MPE) $200–350 + VZP application (free, granted in ~10 days) + accountant $100–200/mo

**Risks:**
- VZP eligibility for AI/ML SaaS is narrower than pure software dev — needs local tax advisor opinion BEFORE committing
- GITA grants have matching requirement (you put 50%, they put 50%)
- Substance requirement: real economic activity in Georgia expected (not just letterbox)

**Action:** Book 30-min call with a Georgian Big-4 or boutique tax advisor (PBServices / Gegidze) to confirm VZP eligibility for Volaura's business model before registering.

### #2 Azerbaijan KOBIA (parallel, not alternative)
**Why:** CEO is AZ-based, local ecosystem grant, keeps "built in Azerbaijan" narrative, runs alongside Georgia without conflict.

- KOBIA (Small & Medium Business Development Agency) — startup grants + accelerator
- No relocation, no new tax residency complications
- Strengthens pitch to local/regional partners (universities, GoDaddy Baku, etc.)

**Action:** Apply to KOBIA in parallel with Georgia setup. Separate legal entity (AZ sole proprietorship or MMC) for AZ-local revenue; Georgia LLC for international SaaS revenue.

### #3 Armenia 1% IT turnover (bridge option)
**Why:** If VZP takes too long or is denied, Armenia is the fastest fallback.

- 1% turnover tax for IT companies (certified regime)
- Same-day online registration possible
- Diaspora/AM-tech ecosystem benefits
- EEU membership — different trade profile than Georgia

**Action:** Keep as documented Plan B. Don't register unless Georgia path breaks.
