## CHECK 2: P2 — ASYMMETRIC TRANSPARENCY

**Standard:** Post discloses something most people hide. Disclosure is surprising, not performative.

**Red flags to catch:**
- Does the post read like a list of "lessons I learned"? (Performative — fail)
- Is transparency selective (surprising what's revealed)? Or exhaustive (oversharing)?
- Does the disclosure serve the post's argument, or does it distract?

**Example PASS:** Publishing AI agent's harsh criticism verbatim. Admitting when AI was wrong about pricing.
**Example FAIL:** Narrating every internal disagreement. Listing all mistakes with a "growth mindset" framing.

**Result:**
- [ ] ✅ **PASS** — Transparency is selective and surprising (not performative)
- [ ] ⚠️ **CONDITIONAL** — Some oversharing. Can be removed without weakening post
- [ ] ❌ **FAIL** — Reads as "lessons I learned" list or excessive oversharing

## CHECK 3: P3 — STRUCTURAL OBSERVATION, NOT PERSONAL GRIEVANCE

**Standard:** Post names a broken SYSTEM. Never names or implies specific people.

**Red flags to catch:**
- Does any sentence name a person or organization (even anonymously)?
- Could any sentence be read as a complaint about a specific past employer?
- Is the tone: "This system is broken" (good) or "Those people messed up" (bad)?
- Could management at a specific organization feel personally called out?

**Pattern check:**
- ✅ GOOD: "At major events, coordinator selection is often connection-based, not competency-based."
- ❌ BAD: "The coordinators at [event] were hired because of who they knew."

**Result:**
- [ ] ✅ **PASS** — Post clearly addresses system, not people. Cannot be read as targeting.
- [ ] ⚠️ **CONDITIONAL** — One sentence could be misread as targeting. Can be generalized.
- [ ] ❌ **FAIL** — Post reads as grievance against specific people/organization.

## CHECK 4: P4 — SHOW THE MACHINE

**Standard:** AI involvement is specific and functional. Not vague marketing ("AI-powered," "cutting-edge").

**Red flags to catch:**
- Does post say "built with AI" or "AI-powered"? (Too vague — fail)
- Does post explain HOW AI is used (Claude making architecture decisions, being disagreed with)?

## CHECK 5: P5 — RESPONSE COST QUESTION

**Standard:** Post ends with a question that requires reflection. Not yes/no. Reader must reconstruct a specific memory to answer.

**Red flags to catch:**
- Does post end with "What do you think?" or "Can you relate?"? (Zero cost — fail)
- Is the question answerable in one word? (Fail)
- Does answering require reader to remember a specific situation? (Good — pass)

**Pattern check:**
- ✅ GOOD: "Siz iş həyatınızda əlaqə ilə gəlmiş, amma peşəkar olmayan koordinatorla çalışmısınızmı?" (Reader must recall specific experience)
- ❌ BAD: "Do you agree?" (Answerable with emoji or one word)

**Result:**
- [ ] ✅ **PASS** — Question requires response cost (memory reconstruction, reflection)
- [ ] ⚠️ **CONDITIONAL** — Question is close but could be more specific
- [ ] ❌ **FAIL** — Question has zero response cost or is missing
