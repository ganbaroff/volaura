## 1. GEORGIA 🇬🇪 — PRIMARY

### 1.1 Virtual Zone Person status (VZP)
- **Corporate tax on foreign IT income:** 0%
- **VAT on IT exports:** 0%
- **Dividend withholding tax:** 5%
- **Local-Georgian income:** 15% (standard rate)
- **Eligible activities:** software development, maintenance, support, updates; software licensing only if the software is developed in Georgia AND the licence includes substantial support.
- **NOT eligible:** pure IT services without software output (consulting, integration).
- **Employee minimum:** none. Solo founders qualify.
- **Application time:** 5–10 business days after LLC registered. Certificate issued by Ministry of Finance.
- **Compliance:** monthly tax declarations required even if zero.

### 1.2 LLC registration via Power of Attorney (AZ citizen)
- **Can register remotely:** yes, via POA notarized in Baku + apostille at ASAN ($60–90).
- **Georgian agent service fee:** $100–150.
- **Government filing fee:** $78.
- **Total direct cost:** ~$240–320 + bank account opening (free, TBC/BOG, 2–5 days).
- **Timeline:** 1 day for registration, 2–5 days for bank, 5–10 days for VZP = ~2 weeks door to door.
- **No physical presence required** during the entire process.

### 1.3 GITA Startup Matching Grants
- **Amount:** up to **100,000 GEL** (~$37,000 USD at 2.7 GEL/USD). Our skill file said $55K — outdated.
- **Cadence:** twice a year, 2 million GEL budget per batch.
- **Eligibility:** Georgian-incorporated entity ≤ 2 years old, registered with NAPR. Business entity under current Entrepreneurs Law.
- **Matching rule:** grant matches private investment (hence "matching"). Applicant typically provides 10–20% co-financing.
- **Confirmed historical deadline:** November 5, 2025 (round). **May 27, 2026 deadline** mentioned in our skill file — NOT verified in this search; needs confirmation via grants.gov.ge/en/Grants direct check.
- **Time to receive funds:** ~2 months from approval.

### 1.4 Georgia ecosystem ancillary benefits
- Individual Entrepreneur "Small Business Status" — 1% turnover tax (alternative to VZP for simpler setups; not recommended for Volaura because VZP is better at scale).
- Startup visa route exists but unnecessary for VZP — you don't need residency.
- Banking: TBC and BOG both accept AZ passport. No rejection pattern known for IT companies.

### 1.5 Risks & caveats
- VZP activities are narrowly defined. B2B SaaS (Volaura selling to orgs) is typically in-scope IF it's software licensing with support. Pure assessment-as-a-service might be borderline — need a local tax advisor opinion before structuring revenue.
- "Foreign income" test is strict. If Volaura organizations are Georgian-domiciled, that revenue gets taxed at 15%.
- GITA grants require matching private investment. Without external investor commitment, grant eligibility is weaker.

## 2. UAE 🇦🇪 — IFZA / DMCC / Meydan

### 2.1 IFZA (International Free Zone Authority, Dubai)
- **Basic licence cost:** starts at $1,850 (AED 11,900), general trading with 1 visa slot.
- **Typical total:** AED 10,000–30,000 ($2,700–$8,100) including visa, office flexi-desk.
- **Visa add-on:** AED 3,800–4,800 (~$1,030–$1,300) per person.
- **Setup speed:** 24–48 hours once documents are in.
- **Foreign ownership:** 100%.
- **Corporate tax:** 9% UAE-wide since June 2023. Free zone companies retain **0% on qualifying income** up to AED 375,000 ($102,000). Above that, 9%.
- **Qualifying income:** trade with other free-zone companies + international. Sales to UAE mainland → 9%.
- **Personal income tax:** 0%.

### 2.2 DMCC (Dubai Multi Commodities Centre)
- **Positioning:** "Global Free Zone of the Year" multiple years. Premium.
- **Minimum capital:** AED 50,000 (~$13,600).
- **Best for:** commodities, crypto, trading. Volaura is a software/talent platform — IFZA is a better fit, DMCC is overkill.

### 2.3 UAE for Volaura — fit
- Strong: tax efficiency, banking, reputation, investor optics.
- Weak: not B2C-friendly for AZ/CIS users. GCC user acquisition is a different playbook.
- Cost: 3–5× Georgia for registration + office + residence visa costs.

### 2.4 Risks
- Substance requirements tightening — can't be a brass-plate shell. Must show real operations if audited.
- Mainland (non-free-zone) revenue creates 9% liability and complicates structure.

## 3. KAZAKHSTAN 🇰🇿 — Astana Hub

### 3.1 Astana Hub IT Technopark
- **Corporate income tax:** 0% for residents on qualifying IT activities.
- **Personal income tax:** relief for foreign IT specialists engaged through the hub.
- **VAT:** relief on qualifying services.
- **Eligibility:** legal entity (resident or non-resident) deriving income from priority activities, certified by Astana Hub.
- **Application:** preliminary review ≤ 5 working days + commission ≤ 10 working days = ~3 weeks total.
- **Visa:** simplified regime for foreign IT specialists and founders.
- **Ecosystem:** 1,600+ registered tech companies by end-2024; 400+ with foreign participation. 25 new foreign IT companies joined in early 2025.

### 3.2 Grants
- Astana Hub offers acceleration + investment matching, not direct grants of the GITA type. Less money upfront, more demo-day / investor connection value.

### 3.3 Fit for Volaura
- Great for CIS reach (common language, adjacent market).
- Tax benefit is legitimate and substantial.
- Banking for AZ citizens: moderate difficulty, doable.
- Distance from Baku: 3h flight.

## 4. TURKEY 🇹🇷 — KOSGEB

### 4.1 ❌ BLOCKER: Turkish citizens only.
- KOSGEB explicitly restricts Entrepreneurship Support Program to Turkish citizens.
- Grant up to 2,000,000 TRY (~$55K at 36 TRY/USD) + 150K TRY bonus for women/young/disabled/veteran.
- **Volaura / AZ founder — not eligible.**
- Workaround: Turkish LLC with Turkish co-founder holding majority. Not a simple move and complicates cap table.

### 4.2 Our skill file claim
- `startup-registration-finder.md` said "Turkish LLC → KOSGEB $50K (July)" as part of multi-jurisdiction grant stack. **This is wrong as written** — needs AZ citizen to naturalize or take on Turkish partner. Correcting in summary.

### 4.3 Other Turkey programs (open to foreigners)
- Teknopark incentives — R&D tax relief, 100% income tax exemption for R&D personnel.
- Turquality (international branding) — open to Turkish companies with exports.
- None directly replace KOSGEB for a cold-start foreign founder.

## 5. ESTONIA 🇪🇪 — e-Residency

### 5.1 Tax structure (2026)
- **Corporate tax on distributed profits:** 22% (up from 20% in 2024). Rising to 24% on 2026-01-01.
- **Corporate tax on reinvested profits:** **0%**. Key Estonian advantage.
- **Additional personal income tax:** +2% from 2026-01-01.
- **VAT:** 22% (EU standard, applies to EU customers; reverse-charge for B2B EU).

### 5.2 e-Residency setup
- Apply online, background check, ~4–6 weeks.
- Company registration: **15 minutes** once e-resident card issued.
- Annual accounting required.
- 120,000+ e-residents, 33,000+ companies.

### 5.3 Fit for Volaura
- Excellent if plan is reinvest-everything mode (no dividends, 0% corp tax).
- EU-based → GDPR is home court, Article 22 compliance is clearer.
- Payment processor access (Stripe EU, Paddle, Wise) — straightforward.
- **Downside:** banking for non-EU residents is harder than advertised. Wise Business / Revolut Business → yes. Traditional Estonian bank → may refuse AZ passport or request physical presence.
- **Downside:** 2026 personal tax +2% hits if you take board member fee.

## 6. CYPRUS 🇨🇾 — IP Box

### 6.1 Tax structure (2026)
- **Corporate tax:** 15% (up from 12.5% on 2026-01-01).
- **IP Box regime:** effective **~2.5% on qualifying IP income** (80% deduction under modified nexus approach).
- **Non-Dom regime:** SDC exemption on dividends + interest for up to 17 years of tax residency.
- **Loss carry-forward:** 7 years (extended from 5).
- **R&D super-deduction:** 120%, extended to 2030.

### 6.2 Fit for Volaura
- IP Box would apply to algorithm + embedding model IP. Requires genuine R&D substance in Cyprus (developers on Cyprus payroll, invoices, lab).
- Non-Dom: CEO would need to relocate to Cyprus to access personal tax benefits. Not happening.
- Without CEO relocation, Cyprus is a holding-company / IP-holding vehicle, not an operational base.
- Cost: €2–4K registration + €1.5–3K annual compliance. More expensive than Georgia but less than Singapore.

### 6.3 When Cyprus makes sense
- Year 3+, if Volaura has real IP revenue >$500K and wants to optimize tax structure.
- Not now.

## 7. SINGAPORE 🇸🇬 — EntrePass + Startup SG

### 7.1 EntrePass (founder visa)
- **Cost:** SGD 225 (~$170) issuance + SGD 105 application fee.
- **Requirements (at least ONE of):**
  1. Raised ≥ SGD 100,000 (~$75K) from single round.
  2. Backed by government-recognized incubator (Y Combinator, Startup SG Accelerator partner).
  3. National awards / IP filings.
- **Processing:** 8 weeks.
- **Validity:** 1 year initial, 1 year first renewal, then 2-year renewals.

### 7.2 Startup SG suite
- **Startup SG Founder:** SGD 50K grant + mentor (requires accredited mentor partner).
- **PSG (Productivity Solutions Grant):** up to 50% reimbursement on approved tech tools.
- **EDG (Enterprise Development Grant):** up to 50% for core capability, innovation, market access.
- **Most grants require ≥30% local (SG/PR) shareholding.** This is a hard barrier for solo AZ founder.

### 7.3 Corporate tax
- 17% headline.
- Startup exemption: first SGD 100K partial exemption, next SGD 100K further partial — effective rate in early years ~5–8%.

### 7.4 Fit for Volaura
- Expensive to operate ($50K+/year minimum, incorporation + accounting + director).
- Best for SEA market entry, which is not Volaura's near-term play.
- Funding access (regional VCs) is strong. Relevant later.
- **Defer.**

## 8. ARMENIA 🇦🇲 — 1% IT Turnover Tax

### 8.1 Tech startup regime
- **Since 2025-01-01, through 2031-12-31:** eligible tech startups pay **1% turnover tax** on qualifying IT revenue instead of profit tax.
- **Eligibility:**
  - ≥ 90% of sales from defined high-tech activities
  - No overdue tax liabilities
  - Listed in the High-Tech Registry
  - **Annual turnover below AMD 115M (~$300K)**
- **Above $300K:** standard 18% CIT.
- **Salary super-deduction:** 200% deduction on salaries to high-tech staff — BUT excludes foreign nationals without Armenian residence right.
- **IT R&D personal income tax:** 10% (reduced from standard).

### 8.2 Setup
- AZ citizen → visa-free entry to Armenia. Can apply for residence via business registration.
- LLC registration: online, 2–5 days, ~$100.
- Banking: easy for AZ citizens (Ameriabank, ACBA).

### 8.3 Fit for Volaura
- First-year killer combo: **1% turnover tax if revenue < $300K** + low setup cost + AZ passport friendly.
- **Political risk:** AZ-AM relations. Legally fine, but founder optics may matter for AZ market perception.
- **Upper limit:** hard ceiling at $300K forces migration to standard regime. If revenue projection breaks this threshold in year 2, Armenia is a bridge, not a home.

## 9. USA 🇺🇸 — Delaware C-Corp via Stripe Atlas

### 9.1 Stripe Atlas flow
- **Cost:** $500 one-time (covers incorporation + state fees + first year registered agent).
- **Ongoing:** $100/year registered agent + Delaware franchise tax + federal IRS filings.
- **Delaware franchise tax:** minimum ~$400/year for no-revenue companies; scales up with authorized shares / assumed par value.
- **Setup time:** 2–3 days online, fully remote.
- **EIN issuance:** 1–2 weeks via Stripe Atlas.

### 9.2 Tax obligations
- **Corporate income tax (federal):** 21%.
- **State income tax (Delaware):** 8.7% on Delaware-source income (typically none for remote-operated companies).
- **Annual franchise tax:** due March 1. Late fee $200 + 1.5%/month interest.
- **Non-US founder:** foreign owners of C-Corp still file Form 1120 federally. 5472 for foreign ownership disclosure.
- **Tax treaty AZ–US:** exists, prevents double tax on salary/dividends.

### 9.3 Fit for Volaura
- Required if raising from US VCs (standard YC / Sequoia / a16z structure).
- Access to Stripe, Mercury, Brex, US payment rails.
- Significant annual overhead: CPA filings $1.5–3K, bookkeeping $0.5–1K.
- **Best pattern:** US C-Corp as holding parent + operational subsidiary in low-tax jurisdiction (Georgia or Estonia). Common SaaS structure.
- **Premature** for Volaura pre-seed. Revisit when US VC conversations start.

## 10. AZERBAIJAN 🇦🇿 — KOBIA (home jurisdiction)

### 10.1 KOBIA Startup Grant
- **Amount:** up to 20,000 AZN (~$12,000).
- **Cadence:** competitions throughout the year, announced on smb.gov.az / startup.az.
- **Eligibility:** IT / innovation / cybersecurity startups. KOBIA Startup Certificate required first.
- **KOBIA total grant funding in recent round:** ~$1.3M across all projects.

### 10.2 Tax regime for IT residents
- 0% income tax for IT residents (7-year exemption for qualifying companies).
- Reduced social insurance for IT workers.
- VAT: 18% standard; IT exports may zero-rate.

### 10.3 Fit for Volaura
- Home jurisdiction — Volaura already de facto operates here.
- Banking works, legal infra familiar.
- **Grant amount is small** ($12K) vs Georgia's $37K.
- **Use case:** file for KOBIA Startup Certificate for domestic legitimacy + apply for 20K AZN grant as a parallel track. Cost = one application.

## 12. GRANT STACKING REALITY CHECK

Our skill file claimed a "$125K non-dilutive" stack from Georgia + Turkey + Kazakhstan. With correct 2026 data:

- **Georgia (GITA):** $37K — REAL, AZ-founder eligible.
- **Turkey (KOSGEB):** **$0** — blocked for foreigners.
- **Kazakhstan (Astana Hub):** acceleration/matching, no direct cash grant of that scale.
- **Azerbaijan (KOBIA):** $12K — parallel-track.

Realistic non-dilutive stack: **~$49K** ($37K GITA + $12K KOBIA), not $125K.

Additional sources worth researching in follow-up:
- EU Horizon Europe / EIC Accelerator (up to €2.5M) — requires EU incorporation.
- Astana Hub investor-matching events — not a grant but capital access.
- Founder Institute / Techstars / 500 Global — equity-based, not grants.
