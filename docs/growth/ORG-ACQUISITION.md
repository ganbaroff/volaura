# Organization Acquisition & Growth Strategy

## Executive Summary

Organizations are the demand-side of Volaura's marketplace. They have the events, staffing needs, and attestation authority. Acquiring orgs creates a flywheel: events → participant engagement → attestations → improved AURA scores → more valuable talent pool → more org demand.

**Target Orgs:**
- NGOs in Baku (international + local)
- Event organizers (conferences, festivals, forums)
- Government volunteer programs
- Corporate CSR initiatives
- University volunteer coordination offices

**Funnel:**
Awareness → Interest → Trial (Free tier) → Activation (1st event) → Retention → Paid Conversion

**Goals by Timeline:**
- **Pre-Launch Event:** 5 pilot orgs signed up
- **At Launch Event:** 10 more interested (pipeline)
- **Month 1:** 15 total active orgs
- **Month 6:** 50 orgs, 10 on paid tier ($29-99/mo)

**Why:** High volunteer turnover, constant need to vet, international presence (English-speaking, open to tech)

**Examples:**
- Red Crescent Azerbaijan
- UNDP Azerbaijan
- Mercy Corps Azerbaijan
- Counterpart Azerbaijan
- World Food Programme
- UNICEF Azerbaijan
- Local youth organizations (Azərbaycan Gənclik Xidməti)

**Outreach Angle:**
"Stop guessing who will show up. AURA scores predict reliability, engagement, and fit. Your strongest people are verified."

**Pain Points:**
- 30-40% no-show rate on events
- Hours spent manually vetting
- Difficulty retaining strong contributors
- Can't assess competency mix of their event talent pool

**Value Props:**
- Pre-vetted talent pool (reduce manual screening)
- Reliability predictor (AURA ≈ likelihood to show up, perform well)
- Competency matching (find leadership talent, not just hands)
- Participant retention (attestations keep people engaged)

## Outreach Strategy

### Channel 1: Launch Event Direct Engagement (May 2026)

**Why:** Orgs attending the launch event are already interested in volunteer coordination, community, events. Perfect warm audience.

**Execution:**
1. **Pre-event research:** Identify org reps expected to attend (check attendee list)
2. **Booth location:** Position Volaura booth near registration/sponsor area (high org traffic)
3. **Org-specific materials:**
   - Flyer: "Organizations: Revolutionize Your Volunteer Pipeline"
   - One-pager: Use case study, pricing, ROI calc
   - Demo device: Laptop showing org dashboard (not just assessments)
4. **Conversation script:**
   - "What's your biggest challenge with volunteer recruitment?"
   - Listen, then: "AURA solves that. Can I show you?"
   - Demo: Search volunteers by competency, see reliability score
   - CTA: "Free tier for your next event. Zero commitment."
5. **Capture lead:**
   - Email, phone, org name
   - Insert into Salesforce or Airtable (lightweight CRM)
   - Mark: "Launch Event Lead — {Date}"

**Target:** 10-15 org conversations, 5-8 qualified leads, 3-5 trial signups

---

## Onboarding Funnel

Org signs up → becomes paying customer. Clear, step-by-step flow.

### Step 1: Organization Profile Creation
**URL:** `volaura.com/orgs/signup`
**Form Fields:**
- Organization name (required)
- Sector/category (NGO, corporate, event, government, school)
- Contact person name + email + phone
- Website
- Verification: manual review (48 hours)

**Why verification:** Trust. Fake orgs could game the system (accept all volunteers, attest falsely). Manual check prevents abuse.

**Email after signup (within 1 hour):**
```
Subject: Welcome to Volaura for Organizations

Hi {ContactName},

Thanks for signing up! Your organization profile is under review.

What's next:
1. We'll verify {OrgName} within 24 hours
2. You'll get access to your org dashboard
3. Post your first event
4. Start searching verified profiles

Questions? Reply to this email or [join our Slack community for orgs]

Best,
The Volaura Team
```

---

### Step 2: Admin Verification (Internal)
**Process:**
- Check org website
- Verify contact person is real
- Search for org in business registry / NGO databases
- Check for fraud signals (fake email, repeated registrations)
- Approve or reject

**If approved:** Send approval email + dashboard login link
**If rejected:** Send polite decline (most likely: email invalid, org not found)

---

### Step 6: Event Execution & Attestation

**At event:**
- Org staff checks in volunteers (mark "Attended" in dashboard, or via QR code scan)
- Post-event, org reviews performance

**After event, org attests:**
```
Form: "Verify Volunteer Skills"

Volunteer: {Name}
Event: {EventName}

Competencies verified (checkboxes):
- ☑ Leadership (Y/N)
- ☑ Communication (Y/N)
- ☑ Reliability (Y/N)
- etc.

Optional comment: "What made them stand out?"

[Submit Attestation]
```

**What happens:**
- Attestation recorded in `attestations` table
- Volunteer's AURA score re-calculated (org verification adds +3-5 points per competency)
- Volunteer gets email: "{OrgName} verified your {Competencies}!" → See email in [[EMAIL-STRATEGY]]
- Volunteer's profile updated with org logo

## Pricing & Monetization

### Free Tier
**Org can:**
- Post 1 event per month
- Search volunteers (read-only)
- Send 5 volunteer invitations
- No attestations (requires upgrade)

**Goal:** Get orgs using, building habit

**Limit:** Designed to make orgs hit wall fast (need to upgrade for 2nd event, more invites)

---

### Growth Tier ($29/month)
**Org can:**
- Post 5 events per month
- Search volunteers (unlimited)
- Send unlimited invitations
- Attest volunteer skills (unlimited)
- Volunteer management dashboard
- Email support

**Target:** 5-10 events/month orgs (medium NGOs, event coordinators)

---

### Enterprise Tier ($99/month or custom)
**Org can:**
- Unlimited events
- Dedicated Slack support channel
- Custom volunteer reports
- Team members (add 2-5 staff accounts)
- API access (integrate with existing HR/volunteer systems)
- Bulk import volunteers

**Target:** Large NGOs, government programs, multi-site organizations

---

### Pricing Strategy
- **Freemium conversion:** Free tier free for life, but friction drives upgrade (can only do 1 event/month = limiting)
- **Growth tier:** Sweet spot for 80% of orgs (cost: $29, value: 5 events = $5.80 per event)
- **Enterprise:** Custom negotiations (need scale)
- **Pricing power:** Eventually increase free tier limit based on market, but keep free option (network effects)

**Payment:** Stripe monthly subscription, annual discount (pay $300 upfront, save $48)

## Special Strategy: Government & Large Institution Track

For government programs and universities, the sales cycle is longer. Separate playbook.

### Government Approach

**Key differences:**
- Sales cycle: 2-3 months (needs approvals, budget)
- Decision-makers: Ministry level, not just coordinator
- Value prop: "Standardized volunteer credentialing, scale national programs"
- Pricing: Per-seat or per-event (not SaaS subscription)

**Execution:**
1. **Identify:** Ministry contacts via research, LinkedIn
2. **Introduce:** Letter from CEO to ministry director (not cold email)
3. **Meeting:** Present as strategic partnership, not vendor pitch
4. **Pilot:** 2-3 month free pilot (1 program)
5. **Case study:** Document results, publish
6. **Expansion:** Roll out to other programs nationally

**Timeline:** 6+ months to first deal

   - Faster to market (no build time)
   - Maintenance-free (cloud)
