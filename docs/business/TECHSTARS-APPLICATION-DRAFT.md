## Company name
VOLAURA, Inc.

## One-liner (140 chars)
Verified professional talent platform — AI-powered adaptive assessment proves skills organizations can trust.

## What does your company do?
VOLAURA replaces self-reported CVs with verified competency scores. Our IRT-powered adaptive assessment engine measures 8 core professional competencies in 15-30 minutes. Professionals earn an AURA Score (0-100) and badge tier (Bronze to Platinum). Organizations search and discover talent by verified skill, not unverified claims.

Think of it as "credit score for professional skills" — third-party verified, portable, and searchable.

## What problem are you solving?
LinkedIn lets anyone claim "Expert" with zero proof. Organizations waste 30-40% of hiring time on skill assessment because resumes lie. In emerging markets like Azerbaijan, professionals have no standardized way to prove competency to international employers.

$120B is spent annually on recruitment, yet 46% of new hires are considered failures within 18 months (SHRM). The root cause: unverified skill claims.

## What is unique about your solution?
Three things no competitor combines:

1. Adaptive assessment using Item Response Theory (3-Parameter Logistic model with EAP estimation) — the same math behind GRE/GMAT, but for professional skills. Not static quizzes.

2. Multi-layer AI evaluation: LLM-based behavioral scoring (BARS method) with anti-gaming detection, injection scanning, and quality gates. Not just GPT-wrapper.

3. Talent discovery marketplace: organizations search professionals by verified competency score, not keywords. The professional doesn't apply — they get found.

Our provisional patent covers two novel mechanisms in AI agent memory architecture (emotional decay modulation + hippocampal replay for knowledge consolidation).

## Why me? (Founder story)
I'm a solo founder from Baku, Azerbaijan — a market where professionals have zero infrastructure for proving their skills. No verified credentials, no standardized assessments, no way for organizations to find talent beyond personal connections.

I started researching neurocognitive architecture — how the brain stores, prioritizes, and retrieves memories. That research became the ZenBrain framework: a 7-layer memory model with emotional decay weighting. Then I realized: the same principles that make human memory work can make AI assessment systems work. Adaptive difficulty. Emotional context. Decay-resistant skill verification.

I built VOLAURA in 4 months. Solo. No funding. No team. I used AI agents as my development team — an autonomous swarm of 13 specialised perspectives and ~118 skill modules that review code, propose features, and catch security issues before I ship. The entire platform — 128 API endpoints, 810+ tests, bilingual, Stripe-ready — was built for under $500 total.

I'm not looking for money to build the product. It's built. I'm looking for the GTM expertise and network I can't build alone from Baku.
