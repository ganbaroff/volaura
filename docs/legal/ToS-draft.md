## 5. Assessments and AURA Scores

### How assessments work

Volaura uses an adaptive AI-powered assessment engine. Questions are selected dynamically based on your responses using Item Response Theory (IRT). The system is designed to measure actual competency, not test-taking strategy.

### Your AURA score

Your AURA score (0–100) is calculated from multiple competency dimensions. It is an estimate of your verified skill level, not a final judgment of your professional worth.

Score weights are published in our documentation. We may refine the methodology during beta — if we do, we'll notify you.

### Assessment data

Your assessment answers are stored. We use this data to:
- Calculate your AURA score
- Improve the accuracy of the assessment engine (AI training)
- Detect anomalous patterns (e.g., answer fishing, rapid cycling)

We do not share individual assessment answers with organizations. Organizations see your AURA score and badge tier — not your specific answers.

### Anti-gaming

We have systems in place to detect: rapid answer cycling, implausible completion times, and statistical patterns inconsistent with genuine responses. Accounts showing clear gaming behavior may be suspended.

## 6. Organization Accounts

### What orgs can see

Organizations can search Volaura's talent pool. They can see your:
- AURA score and badge tier
- Public profile fields
- Skills you have chosen to make visible

**They can only see this if you have set `visible_to_orgs = true` on your profile.**

If you have not enabled org visibility, you are invisible to organization searches. That's the default.

### What orgs cannot see

Organizations cannot see:
- Your assessment answers
- Your tribe membership or tribe activity
- Any data you have not explicitly made visible

### Organization responsibility

Organizations using Volaura agree to use the platform only for legitimate talent discovery. Using Volaura data to discriminate based on protected characteristics (age, gender, ethnicity, disability, etc.) is a violation of these Terms and applicable law.

## 7. Tribes

Tribes are small accountability groups (3 people). They are an opt-in feature.

### What tribe members see

If you join a tribe, your tribe members can see:
- Whether you have been active recently (activity status — yes/no, no timestamps)
- Kudos you have sent or received (kudos are anonymous — no sender identity is stored)

### What tribe members cannot see

- Your AURA score
- Your competency breakdown
- Your assessment history
- Any specific skill data

Tribes are for accountability, not evaluation. The anonymity of kudos is intentional and permanent — we do not store sender_id on kudo records.

### Consent

Joining a tribe is voluntary. By joining, you consent to your activity status being visible to your tribe members as described above.
