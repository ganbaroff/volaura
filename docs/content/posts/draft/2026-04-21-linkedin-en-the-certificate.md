## POST_CLEAN

At COP29 in Baku, I watched a volunteer named Leyla hold together a logistics crisis in real time.

400 delegates. A scheduling system that had just crashed. Two teams speaking different languages, neither of them hers. She had 20 minutes to reroute everything before the session started. She did it in 14.

At the end of the event, she got a certificate. Same PDF, same signature, same stock photo as the volunteer who handed out lanyards at the registration desk.

I've seen this 2,000 times. I've met volunteers who ran media centers, resolved diplomatic incidents before they made the news, coordinated field teams across 80 locations. Some of them have this work as a single line on their CV: "Volunteer at [Event], [Year]." Most don't have it at all.

This is not a story about ungrateful organizations. The NGOs running these events operate on near-zero margin. They don't have HR budgets. They're not equipped to build professional development infrastructure — they're trying to run a conference.

It's a structural gap. The skills are real. The evidence that they happened is not.

We've been mapping this problem for 7 years. 8 competencies. 90 assessment questions. The goal is simple: a credential that tells you what a volunteer actually did, how well they did it, and what a hiring manager should do with that information.

0 users. Still building. But the problem is not theoretical.
